A Quick Tangent on Materials (and Why You Should Care)
#

I know, I know — you came here for rings, not a metallurgy lesson. But hear me out. We’ve tested a dozen different alloys over the years. Brass looks nice but turns green after six months. Sterling silver scratches too easily. Titanium is strong but hard to engrave. For a comfort fit Catholic ring, stainless steel is the sweet spot.

We also do gold plating (18k or 24k) if your customers want something fancier. Just be aware: plated rings need more care. They’re not indestructible. If you’re selling to a church that buys in bulk for confirmation gifts, I’d recommend sticking with the steel. It’s more practical, and the cross band design still looks sharp.

One more thing — we recently added a rose-gold PVD option. Don’t ask me why. It just sells. Maybe because it matches the rosary beads we produce. Speaking of which, check out our Catholic Rosary Prayer Beads 8-10mm if you’re looking for matching sets. Some of our wholesale clients pair the rings with a rosary necklace for a complete gift package. Works well for baptisms and confirmations.
