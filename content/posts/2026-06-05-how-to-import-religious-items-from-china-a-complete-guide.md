### Navigating Shipping, Customs, and Compliance

Importing religious items from China involves logistics that differ from domestic purchasing. Here are key considerations for Catholic wholesalers:

- **Shipping methods:** For bulk orders (500+ units), sea freight is most cost-effective. For smaller batches or urgent restocks, air freight is faster but more expensive. Use a freight forwarder experienced in religious goods to avoid delays.
- **Customs classification:** Rosaries and crucifixes typically fall under HS code 7117.19 (imitation jewelry) or 7018.10 (glass beads). Ensure your supplier provides accurate commercial invoices and certificates of origin.
- **Import duties:** Rates vary by country. In the US, most rosaries enter duty-free under HTS 9817.00.96 (articles for religious purposes). Consult a customs broker to confirm your eligibility.
- **Religious accuracy compliance:** Some countries require that imported religious items not depict symbols in a disrespectful manner. Work with a supplier that follows traditional Catholic iconography—for example, ensuring the crucifix has a proper *INRI* title and the rosary has the correct number of beads.
