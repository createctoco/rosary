## Why Bulk Orders for Custom Enamel Patron Saint Medals Make Sense

We get calls every week from parish buyers asking the same question: "Can you do custom patron saint medals with enamel color?" The answer is yes — and we've been doing it for years. Not just for churches in the US, but for diocesan gift shops in the Philippines, Ireland, and even Australia.

Custom enamel medals aren't complicated. But finding a supplier who actually understands Catholic requirements? That's where most people get stuck.

### The Real Deal on Enamel Medals

Let's talk about what you're actually getting when you order from us. Our enamel medals use hard enamel — the kind that doesn't chip after a few months in a pocket or on a rosary bag. Soft enamel looks okay at first, but it wears down fast. We don't do that.

You want a medal of St. Joseph with blue enamel background? St. Michael in red? St. Francis with animals? We've done all of them. The mold is carved by hand first, then we fill the recessed areas with enamel color. Each piece goes through a kiln firing process. That's why the color stays vibrant for years.

Some buyers ask about lead content. None. Zero. Our enamel is lead-free, cadmium-free. If you're distributing these to children for First Communion or Confirmation, that matters.

### How Many Do You Actually Need?

Here's the thing about MOQ. For custom enamel medals with your specific saint design and your parish name or church logo on the back, we start at 1200 pieces. That's the mold cost spread out. Below that number, the per-unit price jumps too high for anyone to justify.

But here's a trick some buyers use: order 1200 pieces with a generic saint on front, then add your custom text on back. Same MOQ, but you save on mold changes.

For in-stock designs — we keep about 30 different saint medals ready to ship — you can start at just 12 pieces. Need 50 St. Anthony medals for a feast day giveaway? Done. 200 Our Lady of Guadalupe medals for a parish mission? We can ship within a week.

### Custom LOGO and Packaging

We can put your church name on the medal itself. Or on the little card that comes with it. Or on the velvet pouch. Or all three.

Some parishes want their name engraved on the back of the medal. Others want a sticker on the back of the card. We've done both. The engraving costs a bit more because it's part of the mold. The sticker is cheaper for small runs.

For larger orders — 5000 pieces and up — we can do custom boxes with your parish logo printed on them. That's popular for cathedral gift shops and pilgrimage sites.

### The Knots and Details

A small thing, but it matters: how the medal attaches to the rosary or necklace. We use a jump ring — that's the standard way. But if you want a specific knot style, we can do that too.

For rosaries, we can tie the medal with Peruvian knot or flat knot. The Peruvian knot looks cleaner, sits flatter against the beads. The flat knot is easier to undo if someone wants to swap medals later. We ask customers what they prefer.

Some buyers just want the medal alone — no chain, no cord, just the medal in a bulk bag. That's fine too. We'll pack them in poly bags with tissue paper between layers so the enamel doesn't scratch.
