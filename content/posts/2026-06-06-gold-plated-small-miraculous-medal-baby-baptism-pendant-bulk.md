### A Word on Gold Plating vs. Gold Filled

Don’t confuse the two. Gold filled is a thicker layer bonded to the base metal. It’s more durable, but also more expensive. For a baby baptism pendant that’s worn maybe once a year, gold plating at 3 microns is more than enough. One importer from Brazil insisted on gold filled. We quoted him, but he went with plating after testing samples. He said the plating held up fine after a year of occasional use. So unless your customer expects heirloom quality, stick with plating. It keeps your cost down and still looks premium.
